Paddy rice storage in silobag

Analysis of the variables of the storage process of dry and pre-dry paddy rice in silobags that affect the quality of the finished product.

Explore more

Introduction

After harvesting, the green paddy rice undergoes a drying process in which its percentage of moisture is reduced until it reaches an optimum level that allows it to be properly stored until it needs to be threshed.

Said humidity can oscillate around 13.5% for dry rice and 16.5% for pre-dry rice. Once it is close to these values, it can be stored in metal silos in which its conditions are controlled or in flexible plastic tubes known as bag silos by means of agricultural machinery.

The rice is stored for the time necessary for its subsequent threshing, during this storage time the rice undergoes changes in its physical-chemical properties that affect the quality of the finished product, for which sampling is carried out before bagging and in the extraction of the grains to determine to what extent these properties changed.

Data

The data is recorded in excel format by the quality laboratory when analyzing the samples taken during the process, determining variables such as temperature, humidity, impurity, fungus damage, heat damage, amber color, among others.
